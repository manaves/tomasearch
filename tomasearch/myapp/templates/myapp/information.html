Important information

How was the data collected?

The RNA-seq data used were obtained from the Sequence Read Archive (SRA) database, which contains high-throughput sequencing data.

The downloadable file contains the 310 access numbers of the samples used for analysis. All samples used belong to the tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) and have been sequenced using the Illumina platform.

Different types of tissue were used for the analysis. The largest number of samples came from the root (28.06%), followed by the leaves (20%), shoots (16.13%), and seeds (13.23%). For the rest of the tissues, the percentage is less than 10%. Of the 310 samples used, 32.26% belong to paired-end reads and 67.74% to single-end reads.

Data Processing and Analysis

To process and analyze the sequencing data, a combination of widely used bioinformatics tools and custom scripts was employed. Each software was selected based on its suitability for specific steps of the workflow, ensuring both accuracy and efficiency.

FastP

Used for preprocessing raw reads by trimming adapters, filtering low-quality data, and improving overall read quality.

HISAT2

Applied to align RNA-seq reads to the tomato reference genome, optimized for spliced alignments and transcriptomic data.

SAMtools

Employed for manipulating alignment files, including compression, sorting, and indexing for downstream analyses.

Tablet

Provided an interactive environment to visually inspect alignments and confirm strand-specific information.

StringTie

Used for transcript assembly and quantification, enabling the identification of expressed isoforms and estimation of gene abundances.

Cluster3.0

Applied to explore gene expression patterns through filtering, normalization, and clustering of expression tables.

Python Scripts

Custom scripts supported additional analyses, using libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, NetworkX, GOAtools, and SciPy for data handling, visualization, graph analysis, and enrichment studies.

Statistical Tests

Fisher’s exact test and Bonferroni correction were applied to validate enrichment results and minimize false positives.
